**Date Range Focus:** 1885-1964 (VFA/VFL era at Punt Road for home games)

### Disambiguation
```
# Specify football context (Richmond is common suburb name)

# Distinguish from other Richmonds
"Richmond" AND Melbourne AND football
"Richmond" AND Victoria AND football NOT "Richmond, NSW"

# Use club name
"Richmond Football Club" OR ("Richmond" AND Tigers)
```

### VFA vs VFL
```
# VFA era (1886-1907)
"Richmond" AND VFA AND football

# VFL era (1908 onwards)
"Richmond" AND VFL AND football
```

## Notes

- **OCR variations**: Historical newspapers may show Richmomd, Richnond (OCR errors)
- **Hyphenation**: Historical papers often used "Foot-ball", "Grand-stand"
- **Jack Dyer**: "Captain Blood" - Richmond's most famous player (1930s-1949)
- **VFA to VFL**: Richmond joined VFL in 1908 (same year as North Melbourne)
- **Punt Road still used**: Still Richmond's training base and VFL/AFLW home
- **10 VFL/AFL premierships**: Pre-1990 (more recently 2017, 2019, 2020)
